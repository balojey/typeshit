Every typing tool you've ever used has the same problem. It's boring. You stare at text, you type it back, you watch a number go up. There's no stakes, no tension, no reason to come back tomorrow.

We wanted to fix that.

TypeShit is a 3D space defense typing game. You're piloting a ship through deep space. Enemy vessels are chasing you, and they're launching missiles — each one labeled with a word. Type the word correctly, and your ship fires an interceptor that destroys it mid-flight. Miss it, and it hits you. Miss enough, and you're done.

The core loop is simple. The depth isn't.

There are seven game modes. Survival ramps up until you break. Time Attack gives you sixty seconds to prove yourself. Zen mode removes the pressure entirely — just you and the words, no death. Boss Mode throws armored missiles that take three correct typings to crack. Hardcore kills you on the first hit. Speedrun gives every player the same fifty words and ranks by completion time. And Story Mode — an AI narrator reads you a short story, and the words from the narrative become the missiles you have to type.

Under the surface, there's a spaced repetition engine running. Every word you struggle with gets flagged. It comes back sooner next time. And the time after that. Until you don't struggle with it anymore. You're not studying — you're surviving. But the result is the same. You get faster. Your vocabulary grows. The words you used to fumble stop showing up because you've mastered them.

Multiplayer uses deterministic simulation. Both players receive the same word sequence from a shared seed, so competition is pure — same words, same timing, different skill. There's a full tournament system with brackets, match lobbies, and leaderboards that track your single best score per mode. You can challenge friends, create private rooms, or enter organized tournaments.

The dictionary pulls from over 274,000 English words. You'll never memorize the pool. Every session feels different. And because everything persists to a database, your progress follows you across devices. Exit mid-game — your score is saved. Come back a week later — your word stats are waiting.

TypeShit turns typing practice into something you actually want to do. Whether you're a gamer chasing leaderboard ranks, a professional training domain vocabulary, or someone who just wants to get faster — the game meets you where you are and pushes you forward.

Type or die. Your call.
